Not a bad little bike ride...

One of the dangers of bouldering, especially on a wall that isn't very big but is really steep, is that you can really do a lot of anaerobic activity without getting a good balance of aerobic. This has the disadvantage that, not only do you suck on longer traverses and in routes, but you can get fat.

(Ok, I'm not fat, I know, but 'fatter' - and in any case I see these things as trends rather than absolutes. It might be nice that you tell me how skinny I am, but that doesn't mean I'm not snowboarding down that slippery slope to an unhealthy state!)

So yesterday I popped on the bike and whipped out a ~40k ride. Not bad considering it's the second time I've gotten on the bike this year. And I didn't have any water with me. Fortunately it was only around 29°C when I was riding, and most of the time it was cloudy.

It was interesting to see a few parts of Montreal I'd never seen: the Lachine marina, the Fleming Mill with a France-French festival with live music right there, the Lachine lakeshore bike path.

I put a couple of phone pics in the gallery - wow, what a difference a crappy phone cam makes compared to a real camera - and I even recorded a little clip of people surfing at the Lachine rapids, but it'll take more effort to get that online.

I'll have to figure out a way to get the GPS data out of my watch so that I can plot the route.

Afterwards I went up to grab some eats at Euro-deli and hung out with Rob for a bit. On the way home, I had to wade through the Just For Laughs festival. There I saw a number of board games set up by Loto Quebec, a Dancing Table

and a busker or two.

All in all, a nice day. I'm not sure if I'm any less fat, as I immediately felt a need to restore some of the salt I'd lost, but it was really nice.
