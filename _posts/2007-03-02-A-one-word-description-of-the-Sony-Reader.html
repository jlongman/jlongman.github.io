A one word description of the Sony Reader

It's like using a gopher. No kidding, I'm 21 years old again, operating from the CSES offices for pre-livesquid... the FSECG. Basically, you've got a tree, and at the leaves in the tree, there's a document. The pages are slow to bring up, a half second maybe for each. Sounds similar to gopher if you ask me.

There's more (well, any) graphics than that era, but most normal-sized PDFs look terrible on the small (SVGA) screen - they're basically unusable. I admit they would've looked equally as bad on the 13″- or 15″-inch electron cannons we aimed at our faces for hours on days on years on end back in the gopher-era.

I don't know if I should be surprised but RTF's, which date from the same era, look better on the Reader. I still have to check out unformatted TXT files and also some converted HTML to see what they look like.

Some side-lighting would have been nice. Palm Pilots and cell phones and even Newtons got me used to back-lit reading at night while camping without having to use a headlamp.

When it's sitting there, especially at the menus, the display is _sexy_ looking. ~162 DPI is not to be scoffed at. Speaking of the Newton, it's kinda nice to be back in greyscale again, kinda like going home to Pleasantville.

Next challenge: wrest it away from my girlfriend so I can actually read something on it.

(A couple of things I've dumped on it:  ISO standards, TIFF 6.0, Why's Poignant Guide To Ruby, Thinking in Python, some Baen books.  I'll link these  (well the non-ISO ones) later, when it's not 1:30am)
