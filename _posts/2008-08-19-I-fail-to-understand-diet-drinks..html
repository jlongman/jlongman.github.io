I fail to understand diet drinks.

I mean, they taste like crap!

I've long been a big fan of diluting fruit juices and pop with water to bring down the crazy sweetness but still have some interesting flavour. I started when I was trying to drop the carbohydrate component of juice while playing volleyball in high school. I've even drank a lot of sparkling water (until I read that it can remove calcium from your body - which is of course true for all carbonated drinks).

Nothing compares to the crappy taste of diet drinks. They taste like metal. Yuk. Oh, not to mention there's nothing to prove that they actually don't cause weight gain, either directly or indirectly since they've been accused of confusing the body that it was supposed to be getting calories, and yet received none.

  5. kj 08.26.08 / 1pm

    (Nutrition nerd time...)

    I tend to save gatorade for what it's designed for: during and immediately following exercise. As far as I know, In the former case, making the sugar available quickly in the bloodstream won't be followed by the usual insulin response (see for example http://www.ultrunr.com/glycemic.html)

    'The concerns about carbohydrate feedings increasing insulin and thus depressing fatty acid availability are obviated when the carbohydrate is fed during exercise because the exercise-induced elevation in epinephrine depresses the release of insulin from the pancreas.'

    In the latter case, if your muscle/liver glycogen reserve is depleted, and you are ingesting the carb within a short window after the exercise, it will essentially be stored right back in the muscles and liver via glycogen synthesis, again avoiding the insulin response. (the aforementioned article goes into it also, but there are many other studies as well).

    The only real danger, then, sports-wise is consuming a High-GI carb *before* the event, where the resulting 'crash' may make you feel crappy at the start. There doesn't seem to be an effect on performance, though.

    Energy drinks, well, again - I drink ‘em for the caffeine - notably, for a *lower* dose than I would get if I grabbed a coffee instead. That, and I drink the cheap stuff: Red Rain. 0.79 a can. Beats the hell out of the $3 red bull.
