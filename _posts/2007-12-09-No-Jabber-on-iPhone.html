No Jabber on iPhone

So I finally managed to get the toolchain for iPhone building - after re-installing XCode and then installing the occdtools (OCD?) tools from a different subversion revision I even got my apps to compile, not withstanding some random header madness and so on. I then went so far as to download and 'add' Jabber to ApolloIM to see if it would work.

Now it's been an embarassingly long time since I've done much but Java, but either this is extremely well put together code or I clearly missed something obvious or libpurple (an IM library) isn't compiled correctly. I say 'well put together' because adding jabber was trivial: Editing maybe 5 files (slight distinction between jabber and xmpp). Alternatively, I missed something obvious because it didn't work - running my re-compiled version of it from the command line on the iPhone (ha!), well google mail would fail while authenticating, but msn worked fine.

Which could mean something is wrong with libpurple. ref: This and this, from Erica Sadun, although there's a difference between going outside of somethings design and there being something wrong. But for the latter case the interview here suggests that everything is held together by gum and shoestrings... There's some ambiguity, but looking in various repositories it doesn't look like libpurple has been recompiled since September.

(MobileChat 2.0 (beta) is supposed to have gtalk/jabber as well, but ... the comments sound a lot like my experience).

Something I don't understand yet is, why is everything using purple? isn't it an IM library? (NM, my bad, purple != libPurple, thanks Erica!)
