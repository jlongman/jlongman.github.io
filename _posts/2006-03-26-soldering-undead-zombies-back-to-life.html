soldering undead zombies back to life

Spring really is a time of re-birth: My old P3-700 Sony Vaio 505TEK 4lb laptop has been sitting disassembled since November 2005, and today I breathed life back into it.

The sales on the 12â€³ G4 Powerbook that are currently on ($1399 at bestbuy and compusmart) incited me to consider picking one up. One of the primary motivations is that Joan's beast that I'm using, a Presario 900, is heavy, 6.7 lb. The problem is when I side-weight my finger, lifting the laptop, well it, the middle phalanx, feels like it slips out of the joint. Slightly uncomfortable.

So anyways, the problem I originally thought was the power board for the Vaio, a CNX-131. So, I was looking at the over-priced components - overpriced or Out Of Stock - fiddling with the board, looking at the option of just buying a jack which might not fit my power supply... necessitating more money...

Then I realized that the problem wasn't the board - it was the soldering for the jack. When I wiggled the jack, little black outlines danced around the the soldering points of the jack on the board. We-he-heh-heh-heh-ell... I know what to do about that. Dig around the closet for some solder, dig in a drawer for an iron, a soldering iron, jury rig a stand, plug-er-in... Look for my solder-sucker... Nope. Oh well, I'll just see what happens.

Finally I ended up removing the small bit of solder I added as they'd used so much it was a non-issue. Pushed it around and boom. Plug it back in - just to test, and blink! blink! Blink! Yay! Laptop reborn! Uh, so now I have to actually put it all back together - hoping I can find and put all the screws back in, in the right order.

And, er, I don't really have a good excuse to get that sweet-sweet-gadget.
