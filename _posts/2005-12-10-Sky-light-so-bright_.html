Sky light so bright...

Primary mission for the weekend accomplished: Putting two layers of plastic insulation in the skylight.

Two layers? Well I'm convinced that, even though we've never really had any seepage from rain, there's a hole in the skylight or the (non-functional) fan at the apex. By eliminating the convective action with the outside air, I think the R-value should be improved.

But really I think skylights should be easily sealable at night, by design. Something along the lines of an insulative material in a frame, on a track, which slides into place with a long rod. While I might miss seeing moonlight in the winter, the energy savings would more than justify an LED night light.

I also think that skylights should have an external passive summer shield which is designed to block the sun during the summer (i.e. at high angles). Much better than my internal implementation.

By the way, I'm quite happy to say that even though it has dropped to -10°C, we haven't turned on the electric heaters yet. This is due to two things: the water radiators have been working almost non-stop so far this year, and the big warm blanket.

Finally, you'll note that cork has a high R-value. I was shopping for a large mouse mat to replace the 11″x17″ Discreet mouse mat which I can't find (I suspect my former sub-letters nicked it). I was at a local hardware store, and found a roll of cork which is designed to fit on shelves and in drawers to protect glasses and keep items from sliding around overly.

It's fragile - I ended up taping the borders down to protect the sides rather than keep it in place - but its a brilliant alternative to polyethylene, and probably healthier too, less outgassing. It's not exactly smooth, but I like it. It's warm, I don't feel like the desk is sucking my life-force, and my optical meese like it.
