Testing, testing, 1.2.3.

A couple of things are going on here – 1 st and foremost, I've changed to the Hemingway theme. I'm not sure if I'll stay with it as I'm not convinced I can get content across with its layout but it's quite striking, I find.

This was prompted by Kev upgrading my installation of Wordpress to 2.0.4 – it may be that what he did I could have done myself. From what I see when I look at the directories, it certainly does seem that way. But I thought he'd do some magic that allowed him to keep everyone up to date at the same time.

Also happening here is the use of Textfile as a markup tool. Using 'confluence' at work now, so I've gotten use to Textile-style markup. The only thing is the url markup, which I prefer in confluence. e.g. in confluence [plain text|url|title=foo], in Textile, "plain text(foo)":url. It's probably something I can handle, just unfortunate that I have to attend some neurons to one, and fire a few synapses to use the other. Oh, my, dendrites.

Another essential feature, which incidentally prompted the flurry of activity, is I had gotten sick of accidentally deep-sixing a post because I had clicked on a link in the preview for testing purposes, or had accidentally refreshed the page because I do that sometimes out of habit, or am too fast while switching windows in Firefox on a Mac (cause it's really well implemented – option-propeller-[left|right arrow]). In any case, wordpress now thoughtfully puts up a dialog confirming that I want to trash the entry. Confluence's behaviour of saving a draft would also have been acceptable, maybe preferred.

So it remains to be seen what will happen to my gallery most recent pictures, my 'gamercard': and my last.fm recently scrobbled list.
