Antec Fusion and MythTV

I picked up a pretty HTPC computer case a month or two ago and last week I finally had time to stick other parts I'd had sitting around into it. The case in question is an Antec Fusion, a pretty case which handles microATX motherboards, with a brushed aluminum face, a volume knob, a 16×2 VFD display, and a lot of heat- and noise-reduction features. Once I get a few glitches worked out then what I'd really like to do is put the two tuner cards into it and make it the master server.

Glitch #1 - while this is a just frontend for watching video recorded on the backend, I'd really like to get it to boot from the network. The Gigabyte GA-7VM400M _should_ be capable of this. It has a LAN boot option in the BIOS and Knoppmyth has scripts to configure the server-side portion. It should be do-able.

Glitch #2 - getting the VFD to work in Linux. I was getting nothing using the venky imon drivers even with the patch required to compile it with a linux 2.6 kernel. The kernel module was recognizing the device, even when I un- and re-plugged it into the external USB ports. So I installed a copy of XP just to test the VFD. That is the only copy of Windows I currently run. (By currently, I mean, have on disk in an operational state, since I'll probably not use it either.)

A quick note - according to the Antec FAQ's you get a garbled screen if you disconnect the power while the USB connection is still connected or vice versa. The correct response is to disconnect or unplug the power supply for a while. It said 5 minutes but 30 seconds did it for me.

Aside: the *only* use I've had for Windows at home over the last two years is testing hardware components for MythTV. It seems HTPC drivers are targeted towards MCE so it's good to throw back to that system just to test whether your led's have died, your VFD is fried, etc. The only other use I can think of is 'hacking' my phone which, come to think of, I've not had success yet with, either, thanks to an encrypted boot rom. Anywho...

Another action required with this little adventure was switching the NVidia MX440 in my master machine into this frontend so that the quality SVideo output would be used on the TV, and the ATI Radeon 9200SE would be connected via VGA to the LCD TV I have.

Quick note, the easiest way to switch between these brands of videocards is

mkxf86config

and then

install-nvidia-debian.sh # or
install-radeon-debian.sh

which worked quite nicely, once I found the correct commands. Incidentally, xf86cfg filled up my root partition with garbage in an XF86Config file. Tricky to find and not very nice.

I guess the next thing to test it by plugging it into the television and then testing whether Joan can watch TV on the new machine while I play the 360 on the new HDTV, while the master backend is recording two shows at once...
