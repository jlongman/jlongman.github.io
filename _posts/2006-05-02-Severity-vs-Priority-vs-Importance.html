Severity vs Priority vs Importance

Jira's rating system has been extremely frustrating since we first started using it. It doesn't help that the words used have small semantic differences which the people whose mother tongue is not English may not understand (nor I explain, in their language!).

And then Atlassian confuses the two terms!

In Jira, an issue is assigned a Priority. But if you check the definition of Priority, it states they consist of Blocker, Crasher, Major, Minor, Cosmetic. Those aren't Priorities, they are Severities! (Well, maybe a Blocker could be a priority. Which doesn't help).

E.g.: If I have a client which is responsible for 50% of my income and they have a Major then it should be higher priority than the 2% client with a Crasher! (assuming no overlap and Ceteris Paribus, of course)

I prefer to use Urgency and Importance - an issue which is a Crasher is Urgent, but maybe because the 50%-customer doesn't care, it's not Important. Etc.

Atlassian's issue, specifically here, explains why they simplified the system to not having two levels to track, but doesn't detail why they mis-named (or mis-defined) the field. They also don't go into why management would be too stupid to understand the difference.

The solution provided in the issue is to create a Custom Field, something Jira does quite well. The problem I have with that is the terrible definition will persist in the help and documentation, continually bringing up an awful issue. And also I think that given multiple clients it's worth tracking EACH clients priority with an issue.

Finally, its not uncommon to produce a composite index which consists of the product of Importance and Urgency - something I would like to call Priority - which allows a business to keep developing for the future even if a 50%-customer dominates current sales. And taking this further the Priority could slide with age - in an attempt to prevent issue starvation.

(At this point I think we've re-invented kernel scheduling algorithms.)

    (chuckle) My approach has always been, and remains, to establish a severity (or impact) as a rough measure of 'how much' an issue matters to customers/users and to set priorities (what gets worked on at the expense of what) during some sort of review, even an informal one. A natural consequence of this is that the two are not algorithmically linked; determining priorities is precisely what human managers are employed for in the first place!

    I do like the fact that attempting to algorithmically link the two has the dual effects of (a) not doing so and (b) introducing a third abstraction.
