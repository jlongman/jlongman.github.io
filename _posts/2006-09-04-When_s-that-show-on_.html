When's that show on?

I made a small modification to the MythWeb app. It's stupid, it's simple, it hardly seemed worth doing. But it was (almost) pretty easy. All it does it allow you to download an iCalendar file associated with your different tv shows. It seems kinda pointless considering you can tell myth to record the shows, and then you'll watch â€˜em later, but I figure it's actually a nice to have.

Having the iCal item means you can put it in your calendar, or share it with other people. This might be handy to just communicate that a show has been recorded or it can be used to highlight that conflicts need to be negotiated. Anyways, once I figured out how php works (I always thought it WAS perl), wrote (and rewrote, since I accidentally deleted it) a simple RFC-2445 format date conversion tool, figured out how Myth works, found, used and corrected some strange variable reference that looks like a dangerous dangling useless variable (Q: what's the difference between $show and $program? It doesn't work. But I know why it doesn't work. For some reason in the .ics file transmitted from the serer there is a leading blank line and iCal won't accept it. I'm not sure if this is some php weird-ism or something in the iCal library, bennu, I am using.

But that is tomorrow's problem.

(Incidentally, iCal files are accepted in mobile phones, Outlook, iCal, google calendar, shaver's doohickey, and any calendar app worth its salt.)
