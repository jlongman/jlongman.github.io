Spanish dictionary ♥ on iPhone

I don't know why exactly I had this brainfart for so long, but I finally thought, 'surely there must be a spanish<->english dictionary for weDict on the iPhone!'. I was correct, there are MANY compatible dictionaries for many languages.

I guess I thought that someone would make all compatible dictionaries available through online repositories. You'd think that dictionaries would be a higher priority than themes. God knows you've got hundreds of fugly themes polluting the list of entries in the Installer.

In any case, it is trivial to install the stardict-compatible dictionaries:

  1. Download the tarballs and extract them.
  2. Rename the *.dict.dz file to *.dict.gz. Extract the file you just renamed - on OSX I needed to use Archive Utility and not The Unarchiver as The Unarchiver was requiring that I specify a text encoding and then failing without extracting the file.
  3. Finally you need to copy the (extracted) *.dict and *.idx file to your iPhone. Assuming a 1.1.3 or later, it needs to go in your /var/mobile/Library/weDict/ directory. You can either use scp or I have afpd installed, which makes it really easy. You want the mobile user's home directory, and then the ./Library/weDict directory.
  4. Run weDict and look for words you expect to find! Wee! NB: with one of the dictionaries I used (i2e or e2i I believe) if searching in Spanish I needed to add the appropriate accents. E.g. tío (uncle) was not matched by tio. I am testing another Spanish dictionary to see if they normalize sans-accent for utility.

Too bad I did this only 4 days before I head back to the Great White North... It definitely would have made a difference in the speed I would have picked up words! Hasta luego...
