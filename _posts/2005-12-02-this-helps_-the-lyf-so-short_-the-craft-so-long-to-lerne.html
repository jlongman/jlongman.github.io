this helps: the lyf so short, the craft so long to lerne

I've been in a mid-life crisis since going into interviews. There's a certain value to third-party perspective putting you in your place. Let's face it, when I thought, what will I be doing when I'm 32 years old? ... I had no idea what it would be. But now that I'm here, I'm not impressed. It seems to me I sat on my ass too much.

There are too many things I haven't done, code I haven't coded, and thoughts I haven't thought and life I haven't lived.

This, a quick-solution anti-thesis, is the only thing I've read lately that has made me feel any better about it. It has also been the most helpful guide on what steps I could take to get where I want, and helped with the staccato pattern of panicked heart, by bringing my expectations back into line.
