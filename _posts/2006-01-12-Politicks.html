I still have to go through the platform, but I'm pretty sure I can live with it. Some of the things I heard on the CBC this morning sounded reasonable, in at least their spin sounded like the right things to do (e.g. remove subsidies to the (extremely profitable) petroleum industry (I love giving a succesful industry more money); raise taxes on first-stage oil and gas production) even if they never would soon come to pass.

I understand the position that adding taxes to industries is supposed to stifle development. I'd rather say it stifles 'easy development', or lowest-common-denominator development, the kind of industry the US is very good at doing. High taxation and total/full-cost-accounting requires that the industry innovates, something I think North American society is less and less capable of doing.

It would be all well and good if the resources were renewable and the toxic effects of doing easy business were only minimal. But they aren't and it's time the industry, the government and the people grew up and started paying the real price. Because then they might start doing the little things that make a big difference rather than sit back on their increasingly fat asses waiting for their brain to go vestigial.
