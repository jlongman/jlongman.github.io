A weekend in the Glen

Another great weekend with the gang - and Joan came too! - off to the Niagara Glen, cramming 9 people into a 1 bedroom condo, and pulling hard on some limestone (and a touch of sandstone). It was a great time, the weather held together for us and Joan and I had a chance to see John and his new girlfriend Tracey. Add some gamecube Bongos, downtown Niagara, good food at Cafe Tu Tu Tango, some birthday cupcakes and edit: and Easter Eggs! you've got a goodtime. Awesome.

See G's pics and Chris's pics.

The weekend was pretty good climbing-wise: 2 V6's, 'Thunder and Lightning' and 'The Life O'Reilly', and I was close on the V7 sds of 'Life OReilly' when time ran out. Definitely the hardest I've ever bouldered, as far as grades. Except I think those grades are soft - I'm convinced when we were in shape before 'Ye Greate Stoppage Of Climbinge' we could have done them too and I've never sent a V6 before. I'm not certain the 'V7″ was really a grade harder, or maybe just two more V6 moves, IMHO. My impressions of the V6's at Hp40 was they were barely attainable, and the V7s I could barely touch.

Bah, I guess its pretty meaningless anyways - with temperature and humidity variations today's V5 can be tomorrow's V10 and vice versa. I also suspect that as limestone becomes patina'd the grades will move up and as individual climbers get stronger they will downgrade hard climbs. Eventually everything becomes a V4.

And grades are really just guides to how hard the problems are - stars are what we really should be counting. How fun or classic or pure a problem is isn't reflected in the grade.

  3. DaveH 04.24.06 / 8am

    I'm all about the classics. My climbing goal has always been, and will continue to be, to climb at a level where I can do as many 'classics' as I can.

    I am getting closer. As the grades get harder less people climb at that level; as a result less attempts on fewer problems produce less classics at those grades.

    Most of all I like climbs that are naturally geometric with few actual 'holds'. Climbs like The Pondering, Great White, and Mortal Kombat are wicked examples of straight up geometry.

    Another factor is the homogeny. Climbs like Orange Crush, Boomerang and The Millipede Boulder are climbs I consider more enjoyable due to the commonality of the movements && || holds it has.
