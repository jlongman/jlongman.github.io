blinkenlights

A couple of times I've been screwing with the settings on MythTV and wanted to reboot - but I didn't know if the tuners were currently recording. Three ways to check -

  1. in the frontend's UI, go to the Information Center and check the status of the tuners,
  2. start Watching TV, turn on the PIP and if you get both pictures, both tuners are free (I have two),
  3. go to my MythWeb website and check the status.

None of these ways are fast or efficient.

So I was thinking of slapping some coin down on a nice VFD multi-line display, controllable by LCDProc, which MythTV supports. But there was no instant gratification there. And it occurred to me it was really overkill for what I needed.

Then I remembered a little Java utility I'd seen - a light blinker for the keyboard. Not that that's the first time I'd seen computer controlled Caps Lock, Scroll Lock and blah lock lights, but it was the first time I was surprised it worked - and thus remembered. Java generally is hit and miss when it comes to hardware interaction and I've seen non-Java utilities not work on some machines.

Anyways. So a simple search led to wackyd, a Linux daemon designed to blink the keyboard lights. Excellent. So compile, install, test. Woohoo! My keyboard lights are blinking!

So the next step is to bridge MythTV and wackyd. I can see a couple of strategies.

  1. An external process to monitor the logs of the mythbackend server and trigger the wackyd
  2. A copy of the existing Myth LCD process, listening on the same events but sending its message downstream to wackyd
  3. A driver for LCDProc which is limited to blinking lights
  4. A driver for LCDProc which is maps inputs to blinking lights

Obviously the first sucks, the second is alright and scopes to other myth applications, the third requires MythTV to already handle LEDs correctly and the fourth is probably the most general and the most complex.

I just need more free time!
