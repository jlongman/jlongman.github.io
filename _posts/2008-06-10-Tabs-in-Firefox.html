Tabs in Firefox

I hate when the browser switches tabs for me in firefox - in general, yes, when the dns is slow like in this bell-hole, doubly so. It's not enough to use the Preferences pane and deselecting the 'when I open a link in a new tab, open it in the background'.

So about:config in Firefox to the rescue. In about:config, search on tab and set these values to true, (well, per your taste):

browser.search.openintab
browser.tabs.loadBookmarksInBackground
browser.tabs.loadDivertedInBackground

If you use google reader and are sick of switching to the tab which just opened the link then browser.tabs.loadDivertedInBackground is the one for you. However I could see browser.search.openintab and browser.tabs.loadBooksmarksInBackground as something I might eventually set back to false.

Double kudos to Mozilla for making cut and paste work well from the about:config page. (i.e. control-c grabs 'browser.tabs.loadDivertedInBackground;true' and I removed the semi-colon and the rest of the suffix for presentation value).
