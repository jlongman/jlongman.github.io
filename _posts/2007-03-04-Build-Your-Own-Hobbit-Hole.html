Build Your Own Hobbit Hole

While YeeF and I were returning from climbing one evening - something I'm trying to get started again, ever so gently - I mentioned a Hobbit-style house that I'd seen online that I thought was very interesting. But since I've got a swiss cheese memory, I couldn't find it and only came across it again, tonight. 'A Low Impact Woodland Home'.

One of the reasons I find this interesting:

Main tools used: chainsaw, hammer and 1 inch chisel, little else really. Oh and by the way I am not a builder or carpenter, my experience is only having a go at one similar house 2yrs before and a bit of mucking around inbetween. This kind of building is accessible to anyone. My main relevant skills were being able bodied, having self belief and perseverence and a mate or two to give a lift now and again.

There are a number of interesting things about it. It's a roundhouse, built with trees, straw, rock and dirt. It has a green roof and dirt floors. It's low-impact and a lot of the materials were recycled from other buildings or work sites. Lots of cools things to check out.

(As an aside, I found this while I was surfing. I was looking for News of the Weird, but was lazy and typed 'notw'. Which turned up the News Of The World. And I just clicked. It's like car-accident-fascination I guess. Anyways, felicity, serendipity, to whichever it can be attributed, here's the 'hobbit hole'.)
