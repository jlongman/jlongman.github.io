High Definition sickness...

Unsurprisingly, I caught the super-virus going around the world.

Fortunately, my HD ATSC tuner arrived thursday. It's a HDHomeRun box, an ATSC and QAM dual-tuner network-streamer capture thingamajigger... Basically it can receive SD and HDTV feeds, strip out some of the unnecessary or unwanted bits from the Transport Stream and send it to a client like VLC or, more relevant to us, MythTV.

Actually, Friday I stayed home from work sick but then ended up working all day. It's just as well as I postponed the pain.

Anyways, I've learned a couple of things over the weekend:

  • the 0.20 update to the R5D1 release of Knoppmyth wouldn't tune any of the channels that I could see using the command line tool
  • apparently I needed 0.20-fixes
  • which means I'd have to update every version of Myth running on my network, due to protocol changes
  • I compiled it and installed it over top of the afore-mentioned install
    • Using debian packages, so hopefully it's reversible, not including the db?
  • None of the machines I own are powerful enough to decode HDTV except for my MacBook
    • Not the Athlon 2600 I installed in the front room so I can game and Joan can watch MythTV,
    • The P4 2400 with HyperThreading nor
    • The G4 Mac Mini which I had to download a pre-compiled binary for
    • Even the macbook is struggling, but I think it worked well when I was watching it last night
  • Damn it looks good on my macbook, except
  • I have 'sparkles' on my Westinghouse LVM-42w2on all the inputs
    • maybe it's not the same issue?
  • I can't use my laptop while watching HD.
  • I need to decide whether to pull down the plastic weatherstripping from the skylight to move the antenna to improve reception. I'm too dizzy to do that right now.
  • I can detect the encrypted QAM channels but they make MythTV very unhappy.

It's all very cool. But I realized that when you've got a fever you're pretty sensitive to irritation.

And I've got a VOIP box waiting in the background too...
