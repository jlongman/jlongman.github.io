HP 40 redux

(Hard to say this is redux, unless you refer to the last time I was there - and the only record of that that I have is either printed on pictures or on n-year old videotape. I must dig up. Enihoo...)

Inspired by awall's list, these are my from-memory ticks. There's no point in tracking Flashes as I have no recollection of what I've tried in the previous visit. E.g. I know that I tried The Crown many times last time, and this time I flew up it (twice in a row, once without the SDS, since I forgot the start). I'm sure I got on Wasp before too, but similarly, no probs this trip.

Back to the point:

  • High Life Area
    • Circumcision V2
    • Eight Ball V2
    • Chasers V2
    • Short Change V2
    • Wasp V2
    • Wa Wa Wa V2
    • The Crown V2
  • Ten Pins Area
    • Beach V4 - many times, including at night
    • Sometimes V3
    • Copa Cobana (sic) V3
  • Roadside Boulders Area
    • Crisifix (sic) V5 - I think this should be downgraded - really a 'trick' problem
  • Millipede Area
    • Genesis V3
  • Turtle Rock Area
    • Orange Slice V3 - kinda high, including at night
    • Moms V2
    • Ice Cream V1
    • Turtle Head V2

That I did do so well, I think, speaks well of the company I was with - encouraging and helpful. Deech's pointer about toe placement for heel hooks was spot on; working problems with Mel on our own for abit; the other-dave's flash of Sandbox!; watching Kathy be so têteux â€” hmmm, Chris too!; I feel pretty compatible with Pat, despite my height advantage; Jen and awall's long-limbed, low-power, smarts; G - you've just gotten so 'good'. And also the foundation I picked up from John, back in the day.

However, upon reviewing this list, I feel I played it conservatively. 16 problems over 6 climbing days. (0.5 = rest day but we climbed at night, and less another 0.5 versus those who actually climbed the last Saturday), 2.6 problems a day. The median level is probably V2 which is alright but I would have been happier with V3. Losing all my tips last time, having the elbow problems at Font (and again this time), ripping the skin off the tip of my big toe the first day (don't ask) and just not believing in myself until the end. I'm not unhappy with my performance,and I did respect my limitations, I have to, but I know I can do 'better'.

It would help a lot if my climbing regime (i.e. Allez-Up's bouldering, which is where I am all the time) wasn't so limited. No top-outs to speak of, no mantling at all. My footwork needs work, especially wrt active heel hooks and abdominal strength. My flow still feels awkward. But these things can be addressed.

Generally, I had a hard time coming home - it was so good and fun and easy down there. Also, warm and sunny. Come to think of it, I've yet to reconcile a good trip without having post-vacation blues.
