S-ATA drives

So I picked up a 200 gig S-ATA hard drive last night - kind of amusingly, the place where I got it, reminded a lot of me old haunt in Ottawa - Chiang Computers. Although I have to say, selling Atari's was a lot easier than building machines these days - if it didn't work, send it back! And maintenance consisted of dropping a 520ST from 3 inches, or twisting the machine. (Oh, yes, 'Dunderklumpen!') Good times, good times.

Anyways, so I plug the drive into the motherboard and then start looking for the power connector. Check the website, there should be one... This is the older version of the Sonata - and even though the specs say the True Power 380s should have a SATA connector - well, those must be the specs for the TPII 380s. Losers. Start thinking about plugging another PS I have in there which DOES have a SATA power connector... Can't take the old PS out without removing the Zalman heat bloom thing. I don't want to remove the Zalman, nor the processor. Take out the DVD drive, feed all the power cables out — Stuck!

Kelly Clarkson!

Wait a sec! You can buy 12V molex to SATA power adapters! Urge to kill fading, fading...
